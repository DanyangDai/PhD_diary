Paper coming soon

It took a while to get the very first draft paper ready to share with supervisors. It actually took longer than I expected. Not that I am not working hard on it but sometime it depends on feedback from supervisors. But it is finally happening. I was so excited to share the first draft among my supervisors like a proud mom finally given birth to a child and want to show it off. But learning the entire process of publishing rules and journal selecting make me realize that “given birth” to your manuscript is really just the starting point. To “rise a child” (i.e. getting your paper published in decent journal), there are still a lot to do.

The comments I’ve got at this stage are pretty much regarding to grammar and English writing. I am disappointed in myself and thought I should have done better. But also felt bad for my “baby” to be judged with English problems, even everyone is telling me that it is common and don’t feel bad about it. I am getting it polished using all kind of tools available such as Grammarly and ChatGPT. I am aware that I tend to write long and winded sentences. Cutting them down in a concise way is difficult. Because I put a lot of effort into drafting it up, every word means something to me. But on the other hand, it means nothing to the readers or sometime it confuses them.

This very first draft of manuscript is currently circling around with my supervisors, the next step would be sending it to other collaborators for internal reviews. After that would be submitting to journals. This long and headaching journey is what my future going to look like. The research work and analysis stage is like “pregnancy” where you collect data, develop methods and doing the analysis work. Writing the paper is like the “given birth” stage where you “push” for your manuscript. Polishing and internal peer reviews are like dress up and educating your manuscript. Then prepare for your “baby” for pre-school (relatively lower ranking journals) or high school (higher ranking journals) or tertiary educations (top ranking journals like Nature/The Lancet). And of course the journal processing fees varies as well. Tertiary education tend to costs more.

I guess this is all part of PhD and asking myself if I do want this to be the rest of my life. Doing research is fun and rewarding. But getting rejections and criticism is also part of it. It’s all part of the deal. I don’t have any kids at this stage but I could already imagine what is like actually having a kid. You put a lot of 心血 (heart and blood, meaning pouring your heart and sole) into it and it can be sometime rewarding but also disappointing from time to time.

I hope I could get over of this negative feeling about my manuscript and polishing it to the next level soon.

Work Work Work

Working as a causal RA for nursing home project with other professor and post docs for the Department of Health. The government data are messy and massive. Back to data janitor role. Knowing their data collection process and the final data makes me realize that I would never be unemployed. There are so much improvement could be done for the efficiency and clarity of the data collection and process. Working on the nursing home data was also eye opening for me. As a young person who is taken good care of herself and all her family members are healthy, it just bit depressing to see the amount of quality indicators used to measure how well a nursing home is doing in taking care of old people. Like the number of falls, pressure ulcers, mental ability ect. It’s just something I never thought about. I hope the work we do here would make elder’s life a bit better and easier.

Family time

It was good to finally get to spend time with my mom. She came and stayed with me for a month. I enjoyed living by myself and after she left last weekend, I did felt a bit lonely :( I do miss mom helping me with a lot of the housework. But it was nice to have someone at home and not just myself.
